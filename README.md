# Data-Enginnering-Project
Project Title
VBB Transit Data Engineering and Analytics Platform
Problem Statement
Public transportation systems generate large volumes of real-time data, which can be complex and unstructured. This makes it difficult to efficiently analyze important factors such as delays, route performance, and passenger flow patterns. The lack of structured processing and analysis limits the ability to extract meaningful insights from transit data, which can impact decision-making and operational efficiency in public transportation systems.
Introduction
The Verkehrsverbund Berlin-Brandenburg (VBB) coordinates buses, trams, U-Bahn, S-Bahn and regional rail across Berlin and Brandenburg and publishes large volumes of open data every day: static schedules (GTFS), real-time vehicle positions and trip updates (GTFS-Realtime), stop and station metadata, and service alerts. The data is rich and continuously changing, yet it is not combined into a single analytics-ready source. Our project will engineer this data so that the following business questions can be answered:
•	How punctual is each VBB line, station and time-of-day, and where do delays cluster?
•	Which stops and corridors are the busiest, and how does demand shift across the week?
•	How does service reliability change during peak hours, weekends and weather events?
•	Can the expected delay of a trip be predicted 15–30 minutes in advance?
•	How can historical schedule changes be tracked to measure long-term service evolution?
•	How can the raw VBB feeds — with their non-human-readable formats and need for cleaning — be transformed into a query-ready analytical surface that researchers, operators and the public can use without custom scripting?
